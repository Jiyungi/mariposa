# Semen Analysis Reference (WHO 2021, 6th edition)

These are the **real** lower reference limits used by clinicians to read a semen analysis.
Values at or above these cutoffs are considered within reference range; below = flagged for
discussion. Use these to validate the "his" form and to interpret results in the doctor summary.

## WHO 2021 lower reference limits (5th percentile)

| Parameter | Lower reference limit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Semen volume | 1.4 mL | per ejaculate |

## Collection notes (real, used by labs)
- Sample collected after **2–7 days of abstinence**.
- Analyzed after liquefaction, shortly after collection.
- One abnormal result is **not** a diagnosis — labs typically recommend a **repeat analysis**
  a few weeks later before drawing conclusions.

## Lifestyle factors that affect sperm (for the "his" lifestyle form + tasks)
Real, commonly cited modifiable factors: heat exposure (hot tubs, saunas, laptop on lap),
smoking, heavy alcohol, high BMI, poor sleep, high stress. Sperm take roughly **72 days** to
develop, so lifestyle changes are tracked over ~10–12 weeks, not days.
